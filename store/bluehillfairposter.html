Blue Hill Fair Print
$19.99 US


Product Description

This print commemorates the 100th anniversary of the Blue Hill Fair in 1991. The first fair was held in 1891 and has been a popular destination of locals and tourists alike in Downeast Maine. The fair is known for its beautiful location in fields below Blue Hill, the hill whose town bears the same name. Among the many traditional festivities on the fairgrounds are the yearly oxen-pulling competitions and sheepdog trials, which attract competitors from all over New England. The painting The Blue Hill Fair was completed in 1990. It is one of Paul Alexandre John's most famous works and was reproduced in Paintings of Maine (Down East Books, 2006). The print is 22¼ inches high and 19 inches wide and is printed on high-quality French Paralux stock.
